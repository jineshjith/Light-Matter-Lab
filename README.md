# Photophoretic Force Studies

This repository contains experimental and analytical work related to *photophoretic force*, with a focus on its dependence on light properties and particle behavior.

## Overview

Photophoresis refers to the force experienced by small particles suspended in a gas when exposed to non-uniform illumination. This phenomenon arises due to temperature gradients across the particle surface, leading to asymmetric momentum transfer from surrounding gas molecules.

It plays a significant role in:
- Aerosol physics
- Optical manipulation of particles
- Atmospheric and astrophysical processes
- Micro- and nano-scale force measurements

This project investigates how photophoretic forces vary under different experimental conditions, particularly polarization effects and particle motion data.

---

## Repository Structure

### 1. polarisation_dependence/

This folder contains data and analysis related to the dependence of photophoretic force on the *polarization state of incident light*.

Key contents:
- Experimental measurements under varying polarization (linear, circular, etc.)
- Processed datasets and plots
- Scripts for analyzing intensity vs force variation
- Comparative studies highlighting anisotropic responses

Purpose:
To understand how the orientation and nature of the electric field influence thermal gradients and resulting forces.

---

### 2. BD_data/

This folder includes *Brownian Dynamics (BD) data* and related analysis.

Key contents:
- Raw trajectory data of particles
- Statistical analysis of motion under photophoretic influence
- Diffusion and drift calculations
- Scripts for fitting and modeling particle dynamics

Purpose:
To extract force characteristics from stochastic particle motion and validate experimental observations.

The work in this repository typically involves:
- Controlled optical setups for inducing photophoretic motion
- High-resolution particle tracking
- Data analysis using numerical and statistical tools
- Comparison with theoretical models of photophoresis
